About Me

I recently completed a research-based M.A. in Geography at CU Boulder after five years working in software development, politics, and technology education. Since my undergraduate days as an Urban Studies major at Columbia University, I've maintained a strong interest in people and places, and the interaction between them. I have worked directly with diverse populations in professional and volunteer settings, but more recently I have focused on how diverse demographics are represented in data.

I'm passionate about exploring how data succeeds or fails to represent people accurately. Although I'm interested in many types of population information, I specialize in spatial data analysis. Check out my LinkedIn profile for my complete resume. I am currently pursuing employment in research and data analysis or product management. I care deeply about data quality and context, and excel at communicating between technical and non-technical audiences within and outside of an organization.

I'm a lifelong learner and am always developing new skills to benefit my professional work. In my spare time, I play soccer, occasionally run, explore cities, rescue cats, and battle Denver's altitude to bake cakes that rise.
